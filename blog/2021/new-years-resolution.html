My 2021 New Year's Resolution

Published 27. December 2020

I have a habit of creating goals and failing to complete them. This often boils down to one of two elements. Lack of motivation or underestimating the task. If I keep going as I am now, my future as well as my health (both mentally and physically) will decline. "Discipline over motivation" is an easy concept, and in a lot of cases actually pretty easy to follow as well. It's like running, the hardest part of getting into the habit of running is not the running part, but it's getting on your shoes and getting outside.

I decided to create this list of goals and I have every intention of trying to complete them. The reasoning for some of the goals are explained after the list. But here's a non-prioritized list of some of my goals for 2021:

  • Lose weight (83 kg -> 79 kg)
  • Eat healthier (less junkfood/snacks, more greens)
  • Exercise more
  • Meditate more / practice mindfulness more
  • Go hiking at least 3 times
  • Release the game Brainstuck
  • Start creating educational videos and vlogs
  • Start a company
  • Get a tattoo
  • Stop smoking
  • Party less
  • Wake up 6:00 AM every day
  • Keep my room more clean
  • Spend more time with my family
  • Do more dream journaling
  • Do more cold exposure
  • Increase average grade in most subjects (-3, 00, 02, 4, 7, 10, 12) 7-scale
    • Digital Design and Development (4 -> 7)
    • History of Ideas (7 -> 10)
    • Programming (10 -> 12)
    • Informatics (10 -> 12)
    • Mathematics (currently 7, but I'm falling behind)
    • Astronomy (7 -> 10)
    • Danish (5.5 -> 7)
  • Read at least 6 books
    • The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy - Douglas Adams
    • A Brief History of Time - Stephen Hawking
    • Memories, Dreams, Reflections - Carl Jung
    • Man and His Symbols - Carl Jung
    • The War on Normal People - Andrew Yang
    • The Last Train to London - Meg Waite Clayton
  • Get a more statically defined personality / be myself

This is my New Year's Resolution for 2021. There are goals for mindfulness, productivity, future occupation, saving money, becoming my true self and getting a healthy lifestyle (physically, mentally and in every day life).

Some of the goals might stand out, such as "Get a tattoo". This is something I've wanted to do for a long time, and I've known the design of it for the last 6 months. While this might seem weird as a goal, it's not the tattoo it self that's the goal, but it's the value it gives. For the last many years I've been trying to be more comfortable with myself and who I am, and I'd like to have something that defines who I am and what I think about, while disregarding the opinion of my friends, classmates and even family. The tattoo isn't offensive and won't have an effect on them in any way, but the only thing keeping me from getting it, is the constant thought/fear of "what will others think when they see it?". This is a minor step towards the last goal, "Get a more statically defined personality" and becoming more confident. I do not want to switch masks depending on the person I'm around, this can be seen in simple things such as swearing in front of my friends but not in front of my parents, and other stuff like that. By being myself I eliminate / minimize pressure from the situation, as well as establish independence with my thoughts and feelings in any given situation. The bigger question though, is "who do I want to be?". To this I'd answer somewhere between Fred Rogers, Bob Ross, Terence McKenna and Alan Watts. But an in-depth answer might deserve its own blog post.

Feel free to steal any of these goals or let them inspire you. For a good habit tracker that makes it all a bit more fun, I highly recommend Habitica which turns your tasks into a game that you have to complete in order to upgrade, survive or complete a challenge. I thought it'd be a shame not to mention it, since it's what has gotten me to start reading and doing pushups every other day.
