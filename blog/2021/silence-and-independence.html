Silence and Independence

Published 04. January 2021

For many years now I've thought about myself and who I want to be. Often times though the fear of being too different can make this hard.

Just a couple of days ago when celebrating New Year's eve with friends, one of my friends went to my blog and read some of my New Year's resolutions, to which he rather quickly asked with a look of confusion, "why in the world do you want to meditate?". To this I answered the first thing I thought of, which is "it's healthy", to which he responded he responded with "okay." (By the way, if my friend is reading this, hi!)

This is not entirely true, while it is healthy in terms of stress and other stuff, I like the silence, the calm and just the feeling of the present, which is something I've been trying to focus more on. To control my level of "chatter in the skull" as Alan Watts calls it.

Now, I am just a 20 year-old student, which is probably why I get weird looks from friends when I express stuff like this, since it does not seem to be the main interests at my age. Another good example I have of this is that I like hiking, it's great! I prefer to do it alone though, which several of my friends have been confused about when I've said it, "that sounds boring". When I've hiked with friends, it's been fine and a fun hiking trip, but if I'm going hiking to reset / get a break from stuff (social, work, school), I'm most likely going alone. When going with friends, one of the first questions has often been "should I bring a pack of cards or something like that?", which is fine but not personally what I'm looking for. When I go hiking I'm looking to walk the entire distance, be with my thoughts or no thoughts at all and just be in the present. This is harder when you have to be part of a conversation, where people constantly talk, because most people do not seem to be comfortable with silence and having to entertain themselves.

At its base, being able to be with yourself and no distractions, is practical in survival situations, but in general just a good thing to practice. A great example of this has been 2020 with Covid, a lot of people have gotten depressed because of the isolation and not being able to be with friends and family.

I imagine that a lot of this has to do with me being an introvert, with that however I hope to be more independent and mindful than my friends and family in the long run.
